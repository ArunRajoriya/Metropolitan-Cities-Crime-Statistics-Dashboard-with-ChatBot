❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about the NCRB Crime Analytics Dashboard

🔍
📊 What years of crime data are available? +

The dashboard currently provides crime data for three years: 2016, 2019, and 2020. This data is sourced from the National Crime Records Bureau (NCRB) and covers metropolitan cities across India.

🏙️ Which cities are covered in the dataset? +

The dashboard covers 15+ major metropolitan cities including Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore, Chennai, Kolkata, Hyderabad, Pune, Ahmedabad, Jaipur, Lucknow, Kanpur, Nagpur, Indore, and others.

📈 How is the data updated? +

The data is sourced from official NCRB publications. Updates are made when new annual reports are released by NCRB, typically once a year.

🔢 What does "Indian number format" mean? +

Indian number format uses commas differently than international format. For example: 2,90,53,133 (Indian) vs 29,053,133 (International). We use Indian format throughout the dashboard for better local readability.

📚 Where does the data come from? +

All data is sourced from the National Crime Records Bureau (NCRB), Ministry of Home Affairs, Government of India. NCRB is the official repository of crime statistics in India.

🤖 How do I use the Chat Buddy? +

Click the "🔍 Chat Buddy" button in the navigation menu. You can ask natural language questions like:

  • "Compare Delhi and Mumbai arrests in 2020"
  • "Show me the trend for Bangalore from 2016 to 2020"
  • "Top 10 cities by arrests in 2019"
  • "Gender gap analysis for Chennai"
  • "Male arrest trend all years"
📊 What types of analysis are available? +

The dashboard offers multiple analysis types:

  • Dashboard: Interactive filters for year, city, age group, and gender with multiple chart types
  • City Comparison: Compare crime statistics across cities
  • Trend Analysis: View year-over-year changes
  • Gender Analysis: Male vs female arrest statistics
  • Age Group Analysis: Crime data by age demographics
  • Juvenile Data: Dedicated section for juvenile crimes
  • Government Data: Investigation and disposal rates
  • Foreign Crime: Crimes involving foreign nationals
💾 Can I export the data? +

Yes! The Government Data and Foreign Crime pages have an "Export Data" button that allows you to download the filtered data as CSV files for further analysis in Excel or other tools.

📈 How do I use the Dashboard filters? +

On the Dashboard page, you can filter data by:

  • Year: Select 2016, 2019, 2020, or All
  • City: Choose specific cities or All
  • Age Group: Filter by age ranges (18-30, 30-45, 45-60, 60+)
  • Gender: Select Male, Female, or All

The charts update automatically based on your selections. Use the "Reset" button to clear all filters.

🏛️ What is Government Data section? +

The Government Data section shows crime investigation statistics including:

  • Cases registered by crime category
  • Investigation status and disposal rates
  • Trend analysis across years
  • 139 different crime categories

You can filter by year and crime type to see detailed statistics.

🌍 What is Foreign Crime Data? +

The Foreign Crime section analyzes crimes involving foreign nationals in India, including:

  • Cases against foreign tourists
  • Cases against other foreigners
  • Total foreign crime cases
  • Crime type breakdown
  • Year-over-year trends
👦 What is Juvenile Analytics? +

The Juvenile section provides statistics on juveniles (under 18 years) apprehended, including:

  • Total juvenile apprehensions
  • Boys vs girls breakdown
  • City-wise analysis
  • Year-over-year trends
📄 What reports are available? +

The Reports section provides downloadable crime summaries including:

  • PDF reports for 2019 and 2020
  • Word documents for detailed analysis
  • Crime infographics (JPG images)
  • Pre-generated statistical summaries
💻 Which browsers are supported? +

The dashboard works best on modern browsers including Chrome, Firefox, Safari, and Edge (latest versions). Internet Explorer is not supported.

📱 Is the dashboard mobile-friendly? +

Yes! The dashboard is fully responsive and works on tablets and smartphones. However, for the best experience with complex charts and data tables, we recommend using a desktop or laptop.

🐛 What should I do if I encounter an error? +

Try these steps:

  1. Refresh the page (Ctrl+F5 or Cmd+Shift+R)
  2. Clear your browser cache
  3. Try a different browser
  4. If the issue persists, use the Feedback form to report it
⚡ Why are charts not loading? +

If charts don't load:

  • Check your internet connection
  • Ensure JavaScript is enabled in your browser
  • Disable browser extensions that might block scripts
  • Try refreshing the page
  • Clear browser cache and reload
🔍 How do I search within data tables? +

On the Government Data and Foreign Crime pages, you'll find a search box above the data tables. Simply type your search term, and the table will filter in real-time to show matching rows.

🎯 Who is this dashboard for? +

The dashboard is designed for:

  • Researchers and academics studying crime patterns
  • Policy makers and government officials
  • Law enforcement agencies
  • Journalists and media professionals
  • Students and educators
  • General public interested in crime statistics
📊 What crime categories are included? +

The dashboard includes 139 crime categories from NCRB data, including:

  • Murder and culpable homicide
  • Kidnapping and abduction
  • Rape and sexual offences
  • Theft, burglary, and robbery
  • Assault and hurt
  • Fraud and cheating
  • Dowry deaths
  • And many more...
🔄 How do I reset filters? +

Each page with filters has a "Reset" or "Reset Filters" button. Click it to clear all selections and return to the default view showing all available data.

🚀 Is the dashboard free to use? +

Yes! The NCRB Crime Analytics Dashboard is completely free to use. No registration or login is required. All features are accessible to everyone.

📉 Why is data for some years missing? +

We currently have data for 2016, 2019, and 2020 based on available NCRB publications. Data for other years will be added as NCRB releases new reports. The gap between years is due to NCRB's publication schedule.

Yes! We welcome your feedback. Click the "Feedback" link in the footer to access the feedback form. You can report issues, suggest improvements, or share your experience using the dashboard.
